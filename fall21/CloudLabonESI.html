Project Overview:

Background:  The goal of the Open Cloud Testbed (OCT) is to provide bare metal resources to systems researchers for their work on distributed systems. Often such resources are oversubscribed ahead of conference deadlines for systems conferences (OSDI, NSDI, SIGCOMM, etc.) Elastic Secure Infrastructure (ESI) allows the allocation of additional resources from other clusters (e.g, production clouds or HPC clusters) to meet that demand at times. A significant number of researchers use the CloudLab testbed as a “scientific instrument” for their systems research. In addition OCT already exposes some of its resources via CloudLab to the research community. Currently, this is a static allocation of servers which does not provide the flexibility mentioned above.

To achieve the goal of growing and shrinking the available resources in the CloudLab cluster we propose the “CloudLab on top of ESI project”.

Project Specifics:

The goal of this project is to implement and evaluate the following functionality:

  • OCT resources under general ESI control: In the initial scenario, a complete OCT rack should be put under ESI control. This requires the configuration of ESI such that it controls all resources housed in that particular rack (including the networking resources).
  • Signaling between ESI and CloudLab: ESI should signal to the CloudLab control framework that these new resources are available and for how long they are available. In addition CloudLab should signal back to ESI when resources have been successfully released such that ESI can put them back in the pool of unassigned resources. This will require a solid understanding of the CloudLab and ESI control framework and its interfaces.
  • Once this approach is implemented it should be significantly tested to assure its functionality, usability, and reliability.
  • Stretch goal: Allow the control of individual nodes in a rack to be controlled by ESI, while others are managed under different control frameworks (MOC, NERC, CloudLab, etc.)

In addition, the project team shall document their work and make it publicly available.
